# Comedy Example — Chairs Know the Work

## Base rule

Empty chairs move toward the person who did the work.

## Structure

Successful use.

## Progression

1. One chair moves to the intern after late-night work.
2. During a manager’s self-congratulatory presentation, chairs leave the manager and surround the intern.
3. A chair stops between two contributors according to their shared work.
4. Locking the chairs causes the table to move with them.
5. In a client review, chairs map the real team contribution.
6. The company records contribution accurately; the rule no longer needs to appear.

## Comedy logic

The movement is slow and serious. Social reactions—not speed or destruction—carry the joke.
