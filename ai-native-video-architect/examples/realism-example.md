# Realism Example — The Route Without a Name

## Concept

A rural bus route still technically passes a village, but low passenger counts cause the village name to disappear first from the route map, then from announcements, then from the door-control system.

## Core proposition

Places with fewer people may need public service more, yet become easier for efficiency systems to stop recognizing.

## Mechanism

When a stop falls below the passenger threshold, public-transport systems gradually stop recognizing its name and function.

## Scenes

1. The village name becomes a blank dot on the electronic map.
2. The bus passes without announcing the stop.
3. The driver tries to open the door; the system says “non-stop area.”
4. Villagers display a handwritten sign.
5. The driver stops manually, but the official route remains unchanged.
6. Another village name begins to fade.

## Why native

Removing the recognition mechanism removes the central conflict between statistical coverage and actual access.
