# Comedy Controller

## Core principle

The audience should understand the basic rule quickly enough to predict an outcome; comedy then comes from application, social response, misuse, transfer, or a changed level of meaning.

## Rule compression

For short-form rule comedy:

- explain the base rule in ≤25 Chinese characters when possible;
- one trigger;
- one core response;
- no more than two initial limitations;
- add at most one new limitation per escalation.

The audience can understand the base rule early while discovering limits later.

## Choose a structure

- loophole backlash;
- identity misrecognition;
- group tacit agreement;
- rule transfer;
- successful use;
- power reversal;
- audience misjudgment;
- new social order.

Do not default to loophole → escalation → backlash.

## Two escalation tracks

For each scene identify:

- **joke escalation:** what becomes more specific, surprising, or socially awkward?
- **theme escalation:** what deeper relation, role, or institution is exposed?

Compress scenes that only add another example.

## Harm threshold

Choose before escalation:

- embarrassment;
- mild inconvenience;
- actual loss;
- relationship conflict;
- professional/institutional effect;
- bodily danger;
- life danger.

Typical limits:

- light comedy: relationship conflict;
- workplace satire: institutional effect;
- black comedy: bodily danger;
- horror comedy: may approach life danger.

Reclassify the type when harm exceeds the chosen threshold.

## Avoid all-knowing moral worlds

Decide rule reliability:

- objective;
- behavior-only;
- self-perception-based;
- group-consensus-based;
- biased;
- fallible;
- observer-dependent.

Do not let the world always determine who is morally right.

## Ending pool

- successful use;
- power reversal;
- transfer;
- common adaptation;
- absurd institutionalization;
- character keeps the rule;
- rule ends but habit remains;
- failure that reveals the true need;
- audience-knowledge reversal.

Do not default to a final beat of recurrence.
