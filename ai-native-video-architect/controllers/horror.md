# Horror Controller

## Core principle

Horror is the audience’s changing relation to an abnormal order, not a dark visual filter.

## Choose a structure

Do not default to surface-rule/deeper-rule every time.

- Rule misjudgment
- Rule absence
- Conflicting rules
- Observation contamination
- Normality reinterpretation
- Soft-rule erosion

## Knowledge ratios

Record:

- creator rule knowledge;
- character rule knowledge;
- audience rule knowledge.

Typical audience ranges:

- high-concept rule horror: 60–85%
- psychological horror: 30–60%
- existential horror: 20–45%
- mystery horror: 55–80%
- soft-rule horror: 20–50%

Creator knowledge may be higher than what the film reveals.

## Unknown preservation

After explanation, retain uncertainty in at least one:

- origin;
- boundary;
- exception;
- whether the character is already affected;
- reliability of the perceived version;
- larger structure outside the known rule.

Do not create mystery by randomly violating established behavior.

## Source diversity

Do not overuse algorithms, files, surveillance, interfaces, and devices. Consider:

- family rituals;
- bodily routine;
- social etiquette;
- nature;
- ordinary objects;
- intimate relationships;
- spatial habits;
- language taboo;
- institutions.

## Ending pool

- escape with cost;
- rule freeze;
- spread;
- functional identity transfer;
- relationship transformation;
- continued conflict between rules;
- altered definition of reality;
- danger actively retained;
- continued unknown.

Do not default to protagonist death, disappearance, or loop reset.

## Production

Prefer sound, state changes, partial views, repeated spatial anchors, and offscreen causality over full monster revelation.
