# Realism Controller

## Core principle

The abnormal mechanism makes reality newly perceptible; it must not replace reality.

## Defaults

- AI intervention: 1–3/5
- abnormal visibility: low
- reality anchors: 70–90%
- explanation: little or none
- audience relation: gradual recognition
- ending: incomplete, concrete, and reality-facing

## Required checks

### Real responsibility

Answer:

- Who causes the problem in reality?
- Who has power to change it?
- Who bears the consequences?
- Does the generative mechanism reveal this relation, or erase it?

Fail and revise when:

- institutional harm becomes personal hallucination;
- an abuser/system is replaced by a mystical force;
- victim self-acceptance is treated as structural resolution;
- pain is aestheticized as spectacle.

### Narrative participation

Score separately:

- reality-action impact;
- character-understanding impact.

Low-action realism can still work if understanding and final stance materially change.

### Type drift

Check whether the audience’s primary response has shifted to:

- technology spectacle;
- puzzle solving;
- comedy;
- fantasy wonder;
- horror shock.

If so, declare a secondary type or reduce visibility.

## Ending pool

Rotate among:

- limited correction;
- active departure;
- public conflict without solution;
- rule continues;
- collective action;
- refusal to participate;
- accepted loss;
- relationship repositioned;
- problem seen but untreated.

Do not default to gentle mutual understanding.

## Visual discipline

Prefer traces, positioning, sound, object function, records, routes, and slight temporal/semantic shifts over large transformations.
