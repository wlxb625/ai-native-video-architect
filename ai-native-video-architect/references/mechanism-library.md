# Mechanism Library

Use this as a transformation vocabulary, not a topic-to-effect dictionary.

## Standard mechanism form

`expression target → trigger/tendency → world response → escalation → character impact → closure`

## Mechanism families

### Emotional externalization
An internal pressure changes material, sound, weather, scale, or rhythm.

### Identity drift
Identity is redistributed across names, roles, versions, memories, records, or social functions.

### Psychological space
Space responds to attention, avoidance, memory, or relationship pressure.

### Temporal structure
Time loops, skips, accumulates, forks, anticipates, or charges debt. Avoid generic loops unless the exact temporal behavior is thematic.

### Memory contamination
Memory is restored, generated, personalized, exchanged, or made dependent on observation.

### Trace transfer
Damage, labor, guilt, use, or absence leaves traces on another carrier.

### Causality visualization
Consequences arrive early, causes remain visible, or hidden dependency chains become spatial/material.

### Relationship spatialization
Distance, access, orientation, seating, routes, or object positions reveal relationships.

### Multi-version generation
Different users, media, choices, or observers produce incompatible versions.

### Object memory
Objects retain use, relationships, routines, ownership, or absent people.

### Semantic transformation
Language, labels, classifications, contracts, and interface states alter reality.

### Viewpoint estrangement
Nonhuman, system, object, crowd, or impossible viewpoints reorganize what counts as the event.

### Information manifestation
Scores, omissions, unread messages, hidden labels, metadata, or classifications acquire material consequences.

### Rule erosion
A known order gradually loses stability, scope, symmetry, or meaning.

## Candidate diversity axes

Vary candidates through:

- source: body, perception, object, space, time, information, institution, medium, language, relationship, nature, unknown;
- carrier: body, face, shadow, sound, text, screen, prop, architecture, light, time order, crowd, distance;
- benefit: helpful, oppressive, double-edged, power-biased, shifting;
- symmetry: universal, protagonist-only, identity-sensitive, power-sensitive, user backlash, observer-dependent;
- escalation: quantity, intensity, range, depth, speed, irreversibility, social level, meaning, cost, audience cognition;
- payoff: solve, accept, leave, expose, freeze, transfer, spread, institutionalize, rewrite, continue.

## Anti-mapping rule

Never store or follow mappings such as:

- loneliness = transparency;
- anxiety = shrinking room;
- grief = rain;
- growth = butterfly;
- oppression = giant shadow.

Derive the mechanism from the exact situation and relationship conflict.
