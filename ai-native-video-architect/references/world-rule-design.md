# World-Rule Design

## Choose rule hardness first

### Hard rule
Use when audience prediction is important: rule comedy, high-concept horror, puzzle narratives.

Required:

- one primary trigger;
- one primary response;
- no more than two initial limitations;
- repeatable test cases;
- meaningful escalation;
- ending payoff.

### Medium rule
Use when a pattern should be felt and partly understood, but exact outcomes may vary.

### Soft rule
Use for atmosphere, nature, relationship horror, restrained realism, or works where formulaic predictability would reduce impact.

Define:

- direction of change;
- sensory consistency;
- common conditions;
- wrong conclusions characters may draw;
- what must remain unknown.

## Rule reliability

A rule may be:

- objectively accurate;
- accurate only to behavior;
- based on character self-perception;
- biased by a system or institution;
- observer-dependent;
- different for each person;
- a reflection of group consensus;
- partially wrong.

Do not let the world always act as an all-knowing moral judge, especially in comedy.

## Escalation discipline

Use at most two major escalation dimensions. Prefer meaning change over simple scale.

Weak:

> More cracks appear.

Stronger:

> Cracks move from objects, to relationships, to the character’s understanding of what is being preserved.

## Closure families

- resolved;
- accepted with cost;
- active departure;
- public exposure without solution;
- rule continues;
- rule freezes;
- rule transfers;
- rule spreads;
- identity/function changes;
- rule becomes a new institution;
- character rewrites the rule;
- meaning changes while mechanics remain.
