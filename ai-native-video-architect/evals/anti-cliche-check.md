# Anti-Cliché and Replacement Check

## Common-image cost

Flag, but do not automatically reject:

- transparent person;
- faceless figure;
- desynchronized mirror;
- infinite corridor;
- duplicate self;
- upside-down city;
- particle dissolution;
- butterflies/flowers;
- giant eye;
- time loop;
- body melting.

For each flagged element ask:

1. Why is it linked to this exact situation?
2. What causal role does it play?
3. How does it affect the ending?
4. What prevents it from looking like prior AI imagery?

## Replacement test

Replace the primary mechanism with “person becomes transparent” or another generic effect.

- Meaning unchanged → mechanism is weakly coupled.
- Mood reduced only → AI-enhanced.
- Character actions/end change → stronger AI-driven/native coupling.

## Cross-case repetition

For batches, track:

- abnormal source;
- carrier;
- structure;
- escalation dimension;
- ending.

Single source ≤35% of a batch. Same structure/ending ≤40% of a type batch. These are regression-test heuristics, not requirements for an individual project.
